# Engineering Leadership

*Pipeline placement: **EM Round 3** (Engineering Manager candidates only).*

* **Overview**
  * Assess people leadership and engineering-management judgment — distinct from the technical depth of System Design and the technical taste/fit focus of the HM round.
  * Covers managing and growing engineers, building and running teams, delivery and execution, and cross-functional partnership.
* **Format**
  * **Duration:** 60 minutes total — 10 minutes introductions and candidate questions; 50 minutes for the interview.
  * **Style:** Behavioral (STAR-style) plus hypothetical org/people scenarios; led by an experienced EM or senior leader.
  * **Topics:**
    * **People management & growth** — 1:1s, coaching, performance management, handling underperformance/PIPs, retention and attrition.
    * **Hiring & team building** — sourcing, raising the bar, team composition, onboarding.
    * **Delivery & execution** — planning, estimation, prioritization, shipping under constraints, incident/on-call ownership.
    * **Cross-functional partnership** — working with PM/design/leadership; managing up and across.
    * **Conflict & decision-making** — resolving disagreement, giving hard feedback, knowing when to dive into the technical detail vs. delegate.
    * **Engineering culture & standards** — how they raise quality and ways of working across a team.
* **Evaluation Criteria**
  * Score the six standard dimensions (see [Scorecards](../evaluation/Scorecards.md)); for a manager, **communication** and **design and architecture** (technical judgment) are the most likely to surface — mark **N/A** for hands-on dimensions (code quality, testing strategy) when the round doesn’t probe them. The primary signal is the **leadership competencies** recorded as required notes (see [Scorecards](../evaluation/Scorecards.md)):
    * People management & growth · Hiring & team building · Delivery & execution · Cross-functional collaboration & influence · Conflict resolution & decision-making.
  * **Communication:** Clarity, empathy, structured reasoning; credibility as a leader.
  * **Design and architecture:** Technical judgment as a manager — sets direction and pressure-tests decisions without micromanaging.
* **Level Calibration**
  * **EM (single team):** Owns one team’s delivery, health, and growth; sound hiring and people judgment; partners effectively with one PM/design counterpart.
  * **Senior / multi-team EM:** Runs multiple teams or manages managers; drives org-level standards, cross-team execution, and senior hiring; influences technical and people strategy beyond a single team.
