# HM - Technical Taste and Fit

*Pipeline placement: **External Round 4**, **Internal Round 3**, **EM Round 4**. The final, adaptive round for every pipeline.*

* **Overview**
  * Final, **adaptive** round led by the hiring manager (or senior leader). The HM tailors questions to **gaps and signals from prior rounds** — free to go anywhere across system design, technical taste, deep technical dives, and team & culture fit.
  * Assesses **what to build and why**, engineering taste and judgment, depth on the candidate’s strongest areas, and fit with the team and company.
  * Produces the explicit **level fit** recommendation and a **team & culture fit** read.
  * **External Junior:** may include light systems fundamentals (System Design round skipped).
  * **Internal note:** Include promotion scope; align with performance history and manager input.
  * **Engineering Manager:** weight leadership scope, org fit, and technical taste together; the HM synthesizes the Engineering Leadership and System Design signals and confirms EM-track level fit.
* **Format**
  * **Duration:** 60 minutes total — 10 minutes introductions and candidate questions; 50 minutes for the interview.
  * **Preparation:** HM reviews **all prior-round scorecards before the session** and selects topics that close open questions or pressure-test borderline signals.
  * **Style:** Adaptive — whiteboarding, architecture diagrams, deep technical and behavioral discussion, chosen to fit the candidate and prior feedback.
  * **Topics (HM selects based on prior rounds):**
    * **System design** — architecture evolution (12–24 months), scalability, key technical decisions.
    * **Technical taste** — judgment on design, code, tooling, and tradeoffs; what “good” looks like to them.
    * **Deep dives** — pressure-test a domain the candidate claims strength in or a prior-round gap.
    * **Team & culture fit** — collaboration style, values, ways of working, ownership, mentoring.
    * Execution under ambiguity.
  * **Behavioral:** Conflict with PM/design; being wrong technically; driving consensus.
* **Evaluation Criteria**
  * Score all six standard dimensions (see [Scorecards](../evaluation/Scorecards.md)); emphasize **design and architecture**, **communication**, and **code quality** (as technical taste / structural judgment). Record an explicit **team & culture fit** read as a required note (see [Scorecards](../evaluation/Scorecards.md)) — not one of the six scored dimensions.
  * **Design and architecture:** Quality of design; decision framework; evolution and ownership.
  * **Code quality:** Technical taste — structural and API choices, judgment, and what they value in good code — not line-level coding.
  * **Cloud knowledge:** Infra choices, scale path, reliability, and cost tradeoffs in the design.
  * **Testing strategy:** How they would validate and roll out the system.
  * **AI knowledge:** AI/LLM integration in the design, if relevant to role.
  * **Communication:** Influence, stakeholder alignment, leadership maturity; clear level-fit and culture-fit signal.
* **Level Calibration**
  * **Junior:** Ownership potential and growth mindset; light design scope; emerging technical taste; team fit.
  * **Senior:** Independent technical leadership on a pod/area; sound technical taste; strong team & culture fit.
  * **Staff:** Cross-team technical direction without org-wide executive mandate; deep technical taste; culture and standards leadership.
